    What is sfumato?

    Sfumato is a research system that solves grade-school math word problems (GSM8K) using a hybrid of two language models that reason in fundamentally different ways:

    Qwen2.5
    An autoregressive (AR) model. Reads left-to-right, writes one token at a time. The "linear thinker."
    LLaDA-8B
    A mask-diffusion language model. Starts with a fully masked answer slot and fills in tokens iteratively, in any order, refining its guesses over multiple rounds. The "global thinker."

    Why 5 branches?

    LLaDA's filling-in process has a stochastic step (temperature 0.7), so running it 5 times on the same problem produces 5 different reasoning traces. We then majority-vote on the final extracted number. When 4 of 5 branches agree on the same answer, voting confidence is high; when only 3 of 5 agree, it's a near-tie.

    What is "commit-LoRA"?

    A small fine-tune (≈14M parameters) trained to fire only during the last 3 of 4 sub-blocks of LLaDA's denoising schedule. Its job is to stabilise the answer-formatting at the end of generation — "Answer: 18" or "#### 18" — without redirecting the underlying reasoning. It's why the cmajc condition outperforms plain cmaj (82.5% vs 79.5%, a +3pp super-additive lift the paper calls "no double-dip violated upward").

    Headline numbers

    • cmaj-v3 = 79.5% on GSM8K-test (5 branches, base model + prefix-robust LoRA, seed 0, N=200).
    • cmajc-v3 = 82.5% on GSM8K-test (cmaj + commit-LoRA per branch, seed 0, N=200; multi-seed mean = 82.2%, σ ≈ 0.3pp).
    • Total compute spend across the entire paper: ≈$3.50 of RunPod 4090 spot.

    What each tag means

    unanimous_correct
    All 5 branches voted the same correct answer. The "easy" cases.
    clear_majority_correct
    4 of 5 branches voted correctly; one diverged.
    near_tie_correct
    Only 3 of 5 — minimum quorum — agreed on the right answer. Voting was load-bearing.
    near_tie_wrong
    3 of 5 agreed but the majority was wrong. Failure mode: branches confidently disagreed.
    unanimous_wrong
    All 5 agreed on a wrong answer. The model has a systematic blind spot here.
    redundancy_save
    At least one branch dissented but the majority still landed correct. Demonstrates self-consistency value.
    commit_lora_repair
    cmaj got it wrong, cmajc (with commit-LoRA) got it right on the same problem. Direct evidence of where commit-LoRA contributes.
